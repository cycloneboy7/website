Avatar

"Avatar" is a 2009 science fiction film directed by James Cameron. The story is set in the year 2154, where humans have depleted Earth's resources and are now mining the rare mineral unobtanium on the planet Pandora. Pandora is home to a humanoid alien species called the Na'vi, who live in harmony with the planet and are connected to it through a neural network called "Eywa". The story follows Jake Sully (Sam Worthington), a paraplegic former Marine who is given the opportunity to become an Avatar, a hybrid of human and Na'vi DNA, that can be controlled by a human mind through a link with the Avatar's body. Jake is sent to Pandora to infiltrate the Na'vi and gain their trust so that the humans can mine the valuable unobtanium. Jake begins to learn the ways of the Na'vi, and falls in love with a Na'vi woman named Neytiri (Zoe Saldana). Neytiri teaches Jake about the interconnectedness of all life on Pandora and their deep connection to Eywa. Jake's loyalty to the humans is tested when he realizes the devastation that their mining will cause to the Na'vi and their home. As tensions rise between the humans and the Na'vi, the military, led by Colonel Miles Quaritch (Stephen Lang), plans to use force to remove the Na'vi from their land. Jake and Neytiri lead the Na'vi in a battle against the humans, with the support of the other creatures of Pandora. Ultimately, Jake is forced to make a choice between his loyalty to the humans and his love for Neytiri and his new family among the Na'vi. In the end, Jake and the Na'vi are able to defeat the humans and protect their home. Jake makes the permanent transfer from his human body to his Avatar body and stays with the Na'vi as one of them, fully committing himself to their way of life and protecting Pandora from any future human invasion. The film ends with Jake sending a message to the humans on Earth, urging them to respect the interconnectedness of all life and to learn from the Na'vi's connection to Eywa.
